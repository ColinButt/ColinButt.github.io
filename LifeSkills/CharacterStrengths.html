AR Game

Match the character strengths with their benefits!

The Character Strengths AR game was developed using Vuforia in the Unity Engine. When combined with the character strengths cards, it encourages encourages students to familiarise themselves with various character strengths and how those strengths help us in their daily lives.
Every round/turn, the app tells players the benefits of a character strength, and asks them to find a poster/card that displays the name of the character strength. By focusing their device's camera on the cards, players can attempt to match a value to a strength and gain points.
This game is very different compared to our others, as it gets players moving and interacting with others while in their classroom.
Using this rule structure, there are many games that cana be played using this app, including:

  • Poster relay: Each team of students take turns moving around the classroom identifying strengths.
  • Collaborating against the clock: Sitting in small groups, students collaborate to identify and scan the correct character strength.
  • Go Fish: Students take turns identifying strengths and building a ‘hand’ of cards they’ve identified correctly. The game encourages students to share with each other.


    • Cards

    This game was developed during Life Skill's already developed Character Strengths assets. They can be used as posters or just cards.

    Beginning

    Each round begins with the game asking a questioning relating to one of the character strengths.

    Incorrect

    Players answer questions by focusing their device's camera on a card. When the camera focuses on the card, it will scan it and determine if the answer is correct.

    Correct

    When the player scans a card, a star appears and the player scores a point. After scanning a card, a button appears onn the bottom of the screen. Tapping on this screen will open up an animation where characters will talk about the strength.

    Round ends

    After the player finds the correct card, the camera is hidden and stops scanning until the player presses the button. This stops players from accidentally scanning cards between rounds.
